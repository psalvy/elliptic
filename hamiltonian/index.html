Hamiltonian Mechanics & the Geometry of Motion

An interactive course on the deepest reformulation of classical mechanics. We start from a single variational principle — nature makes the action stationary — and build, step by step, to phase space, Poisson brackets, Liouville's theorem, and the onset of chaos. Every equation is derived, not asserted, and every claim that can move on screen does.

Modules 1–6 are the continuous-time backbone: the action, the Legendre transform, phase space, Poisson brackets, Liouville's theorem, and integrability versus chaos. Modules 7–10 are a second arc — from flows to maps — that carries the same ideas into discrete symplectic maps, Lie-algebra methods, reversibility, and the perturbation theory of almost-conserved quantities. That arc is designed to bring you all the way to the research paper Zolkin–Nagaitsev–Morozov–Kladov, arXiv:2505.07223 — Geometry of Almost-Conserved Quantities in Symplectic Maps — which the final module maps section-by-section onto what you have built.

    Prerequisites: single-variable calculus and partial derivatives, comfort with $F = ma$, and a little linear algebra (vectors, the gradient). No prior exposure to Lagrangians or the calculus of variations is assumed — both are built from scratch. Modules 7–10 lean on the brackets and action–angle ideas from Modules 4 and 6.
