The McMillan Map Lab

A sandbox for everything in Modules 7–10. You are steering a reversible symplectic map in McMillan form,

$$q' = p, \qquad p' = -q + f(p), \qquad f(p) = a\,p + b\,p^2 + c\,p^3.$$

Dial the force $f$, click to launch orbits, and read off the structure as it forms. The bare tune at the origin is $\nu_0 = \tfrac{1}{2\pi}\arccos(a/2)$ — stable only while $|a| < 2$. Turn on the symmetry lines $\ell_1: p=q$ and $\ell_2: p=f(q)/2$ (Module 9), or overlay the order-0 Courant–Snyder ellipses (Module 7) to see where the linear approximation still holds and where the nonlinearity has taken over.

Things to try. Set $b=c=0$ and sweep $a$ through $\pm2$ — watch stable ellipses become escaping hyperbolae (Module 7). Add a little $c$ and find the KAM circles, then the island chains. Push $a$ toward $2$ (tune $\to 0$) to squeeze low-order resonances into view. Turn on the symmetry lines and confirm every fixed point sits on $\ell_1$. Add $b$ to break the up–down symmetry and watch the portrait tilt — the reversibility survives, but the picture is no longer its own vertical mirror.

When you are done playing, the paper — arXiv:2505.07223 — is waiting. Every portrait you can draw here is one it has something precise to say about.
