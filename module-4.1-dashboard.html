Bonus: The Grand Unification

You have seen the pieces. Now let's put the entire machine together.

The interactive below unites all three representations of an elliptic curve. When you drag the point $\tau$ in the Upper Half-Plane:

  1. The Lattice transforms, changing the fundamental complex step ($\omega_2$).
  2. The Torus instantly morphs to reflect the new geometry, twisting its orange cycle.
  3. The $j$-invariant is calculated via the infinite Fourier $q$-expansion.
  4. That $j$-invariant is mapped back into algebraic coefficients ($a$ and $b$), instantly morphing the 2D Elliptic Curve.
The 4D Shadow: Because a freely chosen $\tau$ creates a complex $j$-invariant, the true algebraic curve actually lives in 4-dimensional space! To draw the curve on your 2D screen, we extract the real part of $j(\tau)$ and dynamically auto-scale the coefficients to project a "real shadow" of the curve. Notice how the curve becomes singular (pinches or breaks) when $j \to \infty$ near the $x$-axis!
